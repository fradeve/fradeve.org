--- title: Altimetry and gravity description: > Satellite altimeter-derived sea surface gradient and ship gravity for an integrated marine gravity field. created: !!timestamp '2011-06-18 10:00:00' --- {% mark image -%} ![Gravity and Geoid Brazil]([[!!images/grav_geoid_br.png]]) {%- endmark %} **Figure.** Integrated models calculated using sea surface gradients and marine gravity data for the Brazilian coast: free-air gravity anomaly (left) and geoid height (right). Sea surface gradients derived from the Geosat and ERS-1 satellite altimetry geodetic missions were integrated with marine gravity data from the National Geophysical Data Center and Brazilian national surveys. Using the least squares collocation method, models of free-air gravity anomaly and geoid height were calculated for the coast of Brazil with a resolution of 2' x 2'. The integration of satellite and shipborne data showed better statistical results in regions near the coast than using satellite data only, suggesting an improvement when compared to the state-of-the-art global gravity models. Furthermore, these results were obtained with considerably less input information than was used by those reference models. The least squares collocation presented a very low content of high-frequency noise in the predicted gravity anomalies. This may be considered essential to improve the high resolution representation of the gravity field in regions of ocean-continent transition.
